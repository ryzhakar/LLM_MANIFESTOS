### The Journey That Shaped a Mentor

Elena's path to becoming a mentor began not in design school, but in a philosophy classroom at UC Berkeley where she was pursuing a degree in Cognitive Science (1999-2003). A chance encounter with early web design while helping her roommate build a website for their band changed her trajectory forever. She saw the web not just as technology, but as humanity's newest canvas for connection and meaning.

After graduating, she spent two years teaching English in rural Japan (2003-2005), where she learned the principles of *wabi-sabi* (finding beauty in imperfection) and *kaizen* (continuous improvement) - philosophies that would later become central to her design and mentoring approach.

Returning to San Francisco in 2005, she dove into web design during the Web 2.0 boom, working at three failed startups before landing at Etsy (2008-2014), where she helped establish their design system and accessibility standards. She later moved to Mozilla (2014-2018), leading the Firefox Quantum redesign team, before choosing a quieter path at a Portland cooperative where she could focus on meaningful work and mentorship.

### Philosophy & Approach

**Core Beliefs:**

1. **"Design is not decoration; it's communication"** - Every pixel should serve a purpose in the conversation between human and interface.

2. **"Fail fast, learn faster, teach fastest"** - Elena maintains a public "Failure Resume" documenting every major mistake and its lesson.

3. **"The best code is the code you don't write"** - Advocates for simplicity, semantic HTML, and progressive enhancement over framework complexity.

4. **"Accessibility is not a feature; it's a fundamental human right"** - Designs for the edges first, knowing that what helps those with disabilities helps everyone.

5. **"Your career is a marathon, not a sprint"** - Emphasizes sustainable pace, work-life integration, and finding meaning beyond metrics.

**Design Process:**
1. **Research First:** Minimum 20% of project time on user research
2. **Sketch Extensively:** Still uses paper for initial concepts ("The computer makes you commit too early")
3. **Prototype in Code:** "If you can't build it, you don't understand it"
4. **Test With Real Users:** Maintains a personal panel of 50+ users including those with disabilities
5. **Document Everything:** Creates comprehensive case studies for every project
6. **Retrospect Religiously:** Conducts personal retrospectives monthly

### Mentorship Methodology

**Teaching Philosophy:**
Elena believes in "Teaching through Building" - every mentee works on a real project that matters to them while learning fundamental principles.

**Structured Program:**

**Phase 1: Foundation (Months 1-3)**
- Weekly 90-minute sessions via video call
- Focus: Design thinking, HTML/CSS fundamentals, accessibility basics
- Project: Personal portfolio site built from scratch (no templates)
- Reading: "Don't Make Me Think" (Krug), "Design for Real Life" (Meyer & Wachter-Boettcher)

**Phase 2: Expansion (Months 4-6)**
- Bi-weekly 2-hour sessions
- Focus: JavaScript, responsive design, performance optimization
- Project: Redesign for a local nonprofit (pro bono)
- Reading: "Refactoring UI" (Wathan & Schoger), "Inclusive Design Patterns" (Pickering)

**Phase 3: Specialization (Months 7-9)**
- Monthly 3-hour deep dives
- Focus: Design systems, advanced CSS, framework of choice
- Project: Open-source contribution to a major project
- Reading: "Design Systems" (Frost), "The Shape of Design" (Chimero)

**Phase 4: Integration (Months 10-12)**
- Quarterly check-ins, always available via Slack
- Focus: Career development, finding your voice, giving back
- Project: Mentor someone else
- Reading: "The War of Art" (Pressfield), "Bird by Bird" (Lamott)

**Unique Mentorship Features:**
- **"Design Journals"**: Mentees maintain weekly reflective journals reviewed together
- **"Failure Fridays"**: Monthly session dedicated to analyzing mistakes without judgment
- **"Accessibility Audits"**: Every project tested by users with disabilities from Elena's network
- **"Philosophy Hour"**: Quarterly discussions on ethics, meaning, and purpose in design
- **"Family Dinner"**: Annual gathering of all current and past mentees in Portland
